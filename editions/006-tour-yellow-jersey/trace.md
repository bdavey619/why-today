**What changed during this edition's construction:**

The most important discovery wasn't in the research — it was in the revision. The revelation block (as named in the OS) was doing the wrong job: revealing rather than opening. The better job is what we're now calling the "unresolved opening" — a sentence that cannot comfortably exist without the rest of the edition following it. "A closed newspaper has been leading the Tour de France for over a century" is technically true, logically impossible to resolve without reading further, and asks nothing of the reader except forward motion. This observation is recorded in PROJECT_STATE.md for confirmation across editions 007–010.
