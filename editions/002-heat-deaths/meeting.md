# Editorial Meeting — Edition 002

**Date:** June 30, 2026
**Shared moment:** Heat dome over the Pacific Northwest — record temperatures forecast for the next six days

---

## Chosen Question

**What is the deadliest natural disaster in America?**

---

## Why It Won

The question creates genuine curiosity because the obvious answer (hurricanes, tornadoes) is almost certainly wrong. The reader suspects there's a surprising answer — and there is. It connects to something the reader is living through: tonight, where most readers are, it will not cool down.

It is specific enough to have a verifiable, astonishing answer. It is timeless enough to outlast this week's heat event.

---

## Rejected Finalists

*(Full rejection documentation in the Edition 002 editorial record)*

The finalists included questions about heat and climate, sports moments from the World Cup, and other shared moments from early July 2026. The heat dome question was chosen because:
- The surprising sentence candidate was immediately strong ("heat kills more than hurricanes and floods combined")
- The shared moment was something readers were about to inhabit, not merely observe
- The timeless concept (visibility determines response) was accessible through the specific surprising sentence

---

## Pre-Research Assessment

**Candidate surprising sentence:** Heat kills more Americans each year than hurricanes, tornadoes, and floods combined.

**Expected emotional register:** Concern / urgency

**Expected carry type:** Attention — the reader may now notice things about heat they didn't notice before

**Biggest research risk:** The data may be more contested than expected.
