# Editorial Meeting — Edition 003

**Date:** June 30, 2026
**Shared moment:** World Cup penalty shootout — Germany eliminated last night, America plays tonight

---

## Chosen Question

**Why does everyone know the safest penalty kick — and why can't anyone take it?**

---

## Why It Won

The question contains its own paradox: the answer is known and the behavior persists anyway. That gap — between knowing and doing — is what makes it more than a sports question. It is a question about human behavior under observation, which generalizes immediately.

It also passes the text message test: "Did you know goalkeepers who stand still save twice as many penalties as goalkeepers who dive? And goalkeepers dive 97% of the time?" — you would text that.

---

## Pre-Research Notes

This edition was designated as a deliberate anti-template experiment before building began. Explicit questions asked before construction:

- Does this need a revelation block? → No. The paradox is stated in the surprising sentence. A revelation block would restate what the reader already understood.
- Does it need section labels? → No. The prose is short enough not to need navigation.
- Does it need a visual? → Yes — but a simpler one than Edition 002. A goal diagram showing save rates by zone.
- Does it need seriousness? → No. The topic has earned mischief. The close should be playful.
- Does it need a coda? → No. The close ("Tonight, if it goes to penalties, watch where the goalkeeper goes. The center is right there.") is actionable, not haunting. That is what the topic wants.

**Candidate surprising sentence:** Goalkeepers who stand still save twice as many penalties as goalkeepers who dive. Goalkeepers dive 97% of the time.

**Expected emotional register:** Delight / quiet smile

**Expected carry type:** Physical behavior — the reader will watch something differently tonight
