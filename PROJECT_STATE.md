## Current Focus

Editions 004–010.

The goal is not to produce seven good editions. The goal is to discover whether *Why Today?* can repeatedly generate the "I never thought about it that way" response across different topics, formats, and emotional registers.

---

## What We Know

**The editorial move works.** Three editions. Three different emotional registers (wonder, concern, quiet delight). All three produced the "I never thought about it that way" response.

**The process is repeatable.** Each edition followed the same five-phase structure: editorial meeting → research → writing → revision → review.

**The surprising sentence is always found during research, not during the meeting.** Both editions where we tracked this closely showed the meeting-stage sentence being replaced by something more specific and more shocking during research. The editorial meeting selects the question; research discovers the sentence.

**A surprising sentence is necessary but not sufficient.** The object must have enough depth to produce multiple discoveries — one per section, not one discovery with supporting evidence. Editions 004 and 005 both had real surprising sentences. Neither had enough material to reward the reader across an entire reading experience. Rejected at Checkpoint 3 (004) and Checkpoint 3 (005).

**An edition is a reading experience, not an essay.** Every approved direction must plausibly support all nine elements: Shared Moment, Hidden Question, Surprising Sentence, Rich Body, Visual, Human Voice, Perspective Shift, One More Thing, and To Continue. If a topic can only support four or five, it is not ready. The question at Checkpoint 1 is no longer "is this interesting?" — it is "can this become a complete Why Today? edition?"

**Every section should contain a reward.** The visual teaches something new. The quote is memorable enough to repeat. "One More Thing" is the fact that makes someone text a friend. If a section doesn't produce a discovery, it doesn't belong.

**Form should follow topic.** Edition 003 deliberately broke Edition 001's template and was stronger for it. The publication is not defined by seriousness, revelation blocks, or reading lists. It is defined by curiosity.

**The emotional register varies — and should.** Edition 001: wonder. Edition 002: concern. Edition 003: a quiet smile. All felt like *Why Today?*. The unifying quality is curiosity, not tone.

**The repository preserves editorial experience.** Not knowledge (the editions do that). Not taste (editions can't transfer that). The documented decisions — what we chose, what we refused, what we almost became — are the compounding asset.

## What We Are Watching

*Observations from editions in progress. Not yet principles — things we're paying attention to.*

**A surprising sentence is necessary but not sufficient.** The object also needs enough depth that each paragraph can produce a new moment of genuine discovery — not simply supporting evidence for the first one. An edition that explains a single discovery, no matter how strong, is different from an edition that keeps rewarding the reader. The test: does each paragraph earn its place by revealing something new, or by proving something already established? *First noticed after Edition 005 was written and rejected at Checkpoint 3.*

**The news may be a doorway, not the subject.** Across four editions, the strongest moments weren't really about the news event itself — they were about a more durable explanation of how the world works that the news event made visible. The July 4th edition wasn't about July 4th; it was about how national memory gets constructed. The geofence edition may not be about geofence warrants; it may be about the permanent cost of the lag between what technology makes possible and what law prohibits. If this pattern holds, the publication's subject isn't today's news — it's the news as a doorway into something that was always true. *First noticed after Edition 004.*

## What We Do Not Know

**What makes a question unforgettable rather than merely good?** We can identify strong questions. We cannot reliably predict in advance which ones will produce the test response.

**Whether the surprising sentence can be identified before research.** Two editions suggest the answer is no — but this is a hypothesis, not a finding.

**Which categories of shared moment produce the strongest editions.** Three data points cover a national holiday, a weather event, and a sporting moment. Not enough to generalize.

**Whether the editorial meeting is doing what we think it's doing.** It may be selecting the *question* more than the *surprising sentence*. If research consistently produces a better sentence than the meeting identified, the meeting's purpose may need to be redefined.

**What the publication's emotional range is.** We have identified three carry types: memory (001), attention (002), physical behavior (003). Unknown whether there are others.

## Operating System Status

**Version 1 — Frozen as of Edition 006.**

The OS earned its current shape through two failures (004, 005) and one strong success (006). The durable improvements that made it in:

- Evaluate finalists as complete products, not just interesting topics (nine-element check)
- Optimize for curiosity rather than understanding (informed vs. curious observation)
- Require multiple independent discoveries in the body
- Require a visual artifact that emerges from the material
- Require an authentic primary human voice — direct quote, not paraphrase
- Survey attention, not just news

For the next five editions: bug fixes only. No new principles unless a genuine failure occurs that the current system couldn't have predicted.

## What We Are Watching

*Observations from editions in progress. Not yet principles — things we're paying attention to.*

**A surprising sentence can produce either understanding or curiosity.** Why Today? appears strongest when the sentence produces curiosity — when the reader immediately wants to know more rather than simply feeling informed. A sentence that resolves itself teaches. A sentence that creates instability discovers. *First noticed when comparing the birthright citizenship sentence against the Tour de France sentence at Edition 006 Checkpoint 1.*

**The unresolved opening.** The block before the body has a clearer job than "revelation": it should be a sentence the reader cannot leave alone. Sometimes that's a paradox. Sometimes irony. Sometimes a compressed impossibility. The common property isn't the form — it's that the sentence cannot comfortably exist without the rest of the edition following it. A revelation tells the reader the answer before they've earned it. An unresolved opening creates forward motion instead. *First noticed when Edition 006's revelation block was replaced with "A closed newspaper has been leading the Tour de France for over a century." Watch editions 007–010: if every strong edition naturally begins with one of these, we've found something fundamental.* Edition 008 is a third instance (Tencent's license clause opening one day before the Reuters report) — trending toward confirmed.

**A shared moment doesn't have to be a single event.** Editions 001–007 all opened from something with a clear starting gun: a holiday, a weather system, a match. Edition 008 opened from a pattern noticed across a week of trade reporting — no single event, just a trend and a coincidence in timing. It passed the nine-element check anyway. *First noticed at Edition 008's Step 1 survey. Watch whether trend-shaped shared moments can reliably support a full edition, or whether 008 got lucky because the trend happened to break sharply in the same week.*

**A fourth-plus emotional register: wonder sharpening into unease.** Prior registers were wonder (001), concern/urgency (002), quiet delight (003), wonder sharpening into irony (006), wonder sharpening into something heavier (007). Edition 008 is the first where the unease is about a strategic actor's *intentions* rather than a physical, historical, or emotional fact. *First noticed at Edition 008. Watch whether "wonder sharpening into X" is becoming its own template — three instances in a row (006, 007, 008) risk becoming the new inherited structure the framework-leading trap warned about.*

**Human voice from a foundational quote, not a contemporary reaction.** When no clean, on-record source is reacting to *this week's* news, a historical or theoretical quote the edition's argument is actually built on can stand in — Edition 006 (Christophe, 1919) and Edition 008 (Spolsky, 2002) both did this. *Two instances. If a third recurs, promote to a Candidate Pattern in `system/PATTERNS.md`.*

- Which "To Continue" rabbit holes do we actually wish we could keep reading?

## Success Criteria for Editions 004–010

By Edition 010, we should be able to answer:

1. **The repeatability question:** Can *Why Today?* reliably produce the "I never thought about it that way" response, or does it require special conditions?

2. **The selection question:** What distinguishes the questions that work from the ones that don't — specifically enough to be teachable?

3. **The form question:** How much does the publication's form need to vary across editions? What stays constant?

4. **The emotional range question:** What is the full emotional palette *Why Today?* can produce? What is outside its range?

5. **The concept question:** Which concepts recur across editions? Are there five to ten core ideas the publication returns to from different angles?

A secondary goal: by Edition 007, at least one edition should feel like a genuine failure — one that teaches us something the successes can't.
