# Patterns

*Recurring structures that consistently produce strong editions. Distinct from heuristics, which are editorial rules. Patterns are empirical — they describe what has been observed to work, repeatedly, across multiple editions. Heuristics are normative — they describe what should be done.*

**A pattern requires recurrence. We have two editions.**

Confidence taxonomy:
- **Observation** — noticed once, may not recur
- **Hypothesis** — described deliberately, awaiting a second instance
- **Candidate Pattern** — appeared in two or more editions without being forced; named and tracked
- **Confirmed Pattern** — three or more instances; can be relied upon when building
- **Principle** — confirmed and foundational; would require strong evidence to revise

Patterns move forward through this ladder only when they recur without being forced. They can be demoted if later editions contradict them.

---

## How to Use This Document

When building an edition, ask: does this situation match a known pattern? If yes, the pattern is a tested solution. If you're solving a problem the publication has solved before, use the pattern.

When reviewing a completed edition, ask: did a pattern emerge that we haven't named? Add it as a candidate.

When a candidate pattern appears for the second time, note the recurrence. Third time: promote to Confirmed.

### C-01 — The Three-Beat Construction

**Status:** Hypothesis (appeared in two editions but may be oversimplified)

**Structure:**
Surprising Sentence → Evidence → Emotional Coda

**Observed in:** Editions 001, 002

**Description:**
The edition opens by stopping the reader (the surprising sentence), then provides the evidence that makes the surprising sentence credible and meaningful (the body), then closes with something specific and resonant that leaves the reader in a felt state rather than a concluded one (the coda).

The three beats are not equivalent. The surprising sentence is the thesis. The evidence is the engine. The coda is the landing. The edition fails if any of the three is missing.

**Complication from Edition 002:** Edition 002 had more than three beats — the overnight low mechanism, the death certificate undercount, the political economy of disaster response, and then the coda. "Three beats" may be too coarse to describe what actually happens. The structure may be better described as: one surprising sentence → multiple evidence beats of variable number → discovered coda. The number of evidence beats is determined by the topic, not by the pattern.

**What it replaces:** The conventional essay structure of Introduction → Body → Conclusion, where the conclusion restates what was already stated.

**Watch for:** Whether "three beats" is a genuine constraint or merely what Edition 001 happened to produce.

**Awaiting confirmation in:** Editions 003+

### C-02 — Enter Specific, Exit Universal

**Structure:**
Specific Question (with surprising answer) → Universal Insight

**Observed in:** Edition 001

**Description:**
The question is specific enough to have a verifiable, surprising answer. The exploration of that answer opens toward a universal idea that the reader can apply beyond today's event.

Edition 001: "Why is July 4th America's birthday?" (specific, surprising answer: Adams predicted July 2nd, the date traveled with the printed document) → "Nations remember what they decided to celebrate, not what actually happened." (universal).

The direction is critical. A universal question entered first ("How does a country choose its birthday?") has interesting territory but no surprising answer. A specific question entered first has a destination — and the universal insight arrives as discovery rather than premise.

**What it replaces:** The universal-first approach, which produces interesting territory but no surprising sentence.

**Test:** Could the universal insight in the exit be stated before the reader experiences the specific surprising answer? If yes, the pattern has been inverted. The universal insight should feel *earned* by the specific answer, not declared before it.

**Awaiting confirmation in:** Editions 002, 003

### C-03 — The Medium Shift

**Structure:**
Prose building toward a comparative or sequential insight → Visual element (timeline, map, diagram) → Prose resuming

**Observed in:** Edition 001

**Description:**
At the moment when the content requires comparison across time, geography, or scale — and prose would require "first... then... next..." construction — the edition shifts to a visual structure. The visual element is not decorative; it is the most efficient way to deliver what the information is trying to do.

Edition 001: Four dates in Philadelphia (July 2, July 4, July 8, August 2) needed to be compared. A timeline made the gap between July 2nd and July 4th *visible*, not merely stated. The reader could see the independence vote and the Declaration's printing as separate things on a spine.

**The diagnostic question:** If you find yourself writing "first... then... next..." or "in one column... in another column..." or "on the left... on the right..." — the content wants to become visual.

**What it replaces:** Prose that describes what a visual would show directly.

**Watch for:** The medium shift must serve understanding, not aesthetics. A timeline that doesn't reduce reader effort is decoration. Ask: does this visual element make the information faster to understand than prose would?

**Awaiting confirmation in:** Editions 002+

### C-04 — The Foreknowledge Setup

**Structure:**
State the surprising sentence → Show the evidence → Reader already knows the ending, so the evidence becomes investigative rather than informational

**Observed in:** Edition 001

**Description:**
By revealing Adams's wrong prediction (the surprising sentence) *before* showing the timeline (the evidence), the reader enters the timeline already knowing that July 2nd was the decisive date and July 4th was merely the date on a document. This foreknowledge changes the quality of attention. The reader isn't absorbing information — they're tracing the path of a mistake. Each entry in the timeline is read in the light of what they already know.

This is the opposite of a mystery structure, where the reader is kept ignorant until a reveal. The foreknowledge setup gives the reader the ending immediately and then shows them how the world arrived at a different ending.

**The distinction from a mystery structure:** A mystery structure withholds information to create suspense. The foreknowledge setup gives information immediately to create a different kind of attention — archaeological rather than anticipatory. Both work; they produce different reading experiences.

**Watch for:** Does showing the ending first make the evidence more or less interesting? In Edition 001, it made it more interesting. This may not be true for all topics.

**Awaiting confirmation in:** Editions 002+

### C-05 — The Discovered Coda

**Status:** Candidate Pattern (2 instances — promote to Confirmed if appears in Edition 003 without being forced)

**Structure:**
Research yields a specific, strange, resonant fact that closes the edition without summarizing it

**Observed in:** Editions 001, 002

**Description:**
Edition 001: "Both Adams and Jefferson died on July 4th, 1826. Adams's last words were reported to be: 'Thomas Jefferson survives.' He didn't." — found during research; not planned.

Edition 002: Eric Klinenberg's finding that the defining variable in the 1995 Chicago heat wave was social isolation, not poverty or heat exposure — found during research; changed the edition's ending and its meaning.

In both cases, the discovered coda did something a constructed coda could not: it introduced a new dimension of meaning at the moment of closing rather than restating what the edition had already established.

**The pattern:** The best codas seem to be ones that could not have been composed from the edition's argument. They are discoveries that the reader experiences as arrival rather than conclusion.

**Implication for the research process:** Look for the discovered coda during research. The question to ask: is there something in this material so specific and strange that placing it last would change what the whole edition meant? Don't construct toward it — discover it or don't use it.

**Caution:** The discovered coda becoming an expected structural slot is itself a template risk. Edition 002 was built with a "coda slot" that was then filled with the Klinenberg material. Whether the Klinenberg material would have found a different (possibly better) position if the slot hadn't been waiting for it is unknown.

### C-06 — The Revelation Block

**Status:** Hypothesis — with a specific caution about template risk

**Structure:**
A single idea — stated with confidence, set apart visually — at the emotional center of the edition

**Observed in:** Editions 001, 002

**Description:**
Edition 001: "Nations don't remember what happened. They remember what they decided to celebrate."
Edition 002: "The disasters we respond to are the ones we can photograph."

Both are real revelations — the moment when the specific surprising answer opens into a universal idea. The visual treatment (dark full-bleed block) marks this moment with distinction.

**The caution:** The Revelation Block appeared in Edition 002 partly because Edition 001 had one. It was not rederived from what Edition 002 wanted. Whether "The disasters we respond to are the ones we can photograph" needs the dark-background treatment to land — or whether it would be just as powerful as a paragraph that earned its position through writing — was never tested. The visual treatment was applied from template, not from need.

**What we do not yet know:** Whether the Revelation Block is a genuine pattern or a structural habit. It is possible that some editions want visual revelation and some want revelation embedded in prose. The distinction between "this edition wants a revelation block" and "we gave this edition a revelation block because the previous one had one" is critical and currently invisible in production.

**Until this is resolved:** Ask before applying the visual treatment — does this edition independently want a revealed center, or is it following a template?

### C-07 — The Edition Photo

**Status:** Hypothesis — deliberate editorial decision after Edition 010 trial; awaiting confirmation across multiple editions

**Structure:**
A single photograph placed between the revelation wrapper and the first paragraph of body content, with a descriptive caption

**Observed in:** Edition 010

**Description:**
Edition 010 introduced a photograph of Teheran-ro (Seoul's corporate corridor) between the revelation wrapper and the body content. The image served not as illustration but as orientation — giving the reader a visual anchor for the world the edition was about to describe. The caption extended that work: "The advertising is a map of who runs the country."

The effect is grounding in place and texture before the argument begins. The reader enters the body content already holding an image, so subsequent mentions of Samsung towers, Gangnam-gu, and Korean street-level culture all land against something they've already seen.

**Editorial standard:** Every edition should include a photograph. The photo does real work — not decoration, but orientation. Ask: does this image give the reader something they need to be in the right place for the edition's argument?

**Placement:** Between the revelation wrapper and the first paragraph of body content.

**Caption standard:** Factual description, then one editorial sentence that gives the image meaning in context of the edition. Source credit (Wikimedia Commons / CC license).

**Source preference:** Wikimedia Commons. URL formula: `https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/[a]/[ab]/Filename`, where `a` and `ab` are the first 1 and 2 hex characters of the MD5 hash of the exact filename. Compute with: `echo -n "Filename.jpg" | md5sum | cut -c1-2`.

**What it replaces:** Pure text editions with no visual grounding.

**Watch for:** Whether the photo consistently increases immersion or whether some editions are stronger without one.
*(Source: Edition 010 — Teheran-ro trial; user: "I didn't realize how much adding a photo increases the richness.")*

## Patterns Under Watch

*(Observations that may become candidate patterns with more evidence. Not yet promoted.)*

**The question evolves through research.** *[Hypothesis — 2 instances]* The editorial meeting selects the question; research discovers the true surprising sentence and often the true angle. Both editions saw the surprising sentence change between meeting and publication. If this recurs in Edition 003, promote to Candidate Pattern and reconsider what the editorial meeting is actually for.

**The living-vs-witnessing advantage.** *[Observation — weakening after Edition 002]* Edition 002 was supposed to instantiate this: the heat dome is something the reader inhabits. But the edition's strength came from the overnight low mechanism and the death certificate insight — both of which would work on any summer day. The timeliness frame was added during writing, not derived from what made the question strong. Watching but not expecting confirmation.

**The rejection ratio.** *[Observation — 2 instances]* Both editions ran approximately 20 questions to select 1. Unknown whether 20 is right or whether it happens to be what the first two editorial meetings produced.

**Manufactured sections feel different from discovered ones.** *[Observation — 1 instance]* Edition 002's political economy section (the "if hurricanes had a tenfold undercount" paragraph) was built from the revelation's argument rather than discovered during research. It was the weakest section. Unknown whether this is a consistent signal or a coincidence. If future editions show the same pattern — weakest sections are always built, strongest sections are always found — this becomes significant for the construction algorithm.

## How Patterns Graduate

| Stage | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Under Watch | Observed incidentally, may not recur |
| Candidate | Observed deliberately in at least one edition, named and described |
| Confirmed | Observed in at least three editions, not forced, and produces consistently strong results when applied |
| Deprecated | Confirmed at one point but no longer reliably produces strong results; retired with explanation |
